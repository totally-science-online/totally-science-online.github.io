    Flash games were a popular genre of online games that utilized Adobe Flash technology, allowing for quick and easy access through web browsers without the need for downloads. Thriving in the late 1990s and early 2000s, these games featured simple graphics, straightforward gameplay mechanics, and a wide variety of genres, including action, puzzles, platformers, and strategy. Titles like Happy Wheels, Bloons Tower Defense, and Super Mario 63 captivated millions of players with their engaging gameplay and often quirky humor. Flash games were particularly appealing due to their accessibility, allowing players to enjoy short gaming sessions while at work or school. Although Adobe officially ended support for Flash in 2020, many of these classic games continue to hold nostalgic value for gamers and can be found on various emulation sites and dedicated game platforms.
