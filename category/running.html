    Running games are a fast-paced genre focused on continuous movement, where players must navigate characters through obstacle-filled environments, often with the goal of surviving as long as possible. These games typically feature simple controls—usually just jumping, sliding, or dodging—while the character automatically runs forward. Popular titles like Temple Run, Subway Surfers, and Canabalt emphasize quick reflexes, timing, and precision as players dodge obstacles, collect power-ups, and earn points. Running games are known for their addictive gameplay loops, offering endless challenges as players try to beat their high scores or achieve objectives. Their easy-to-learn mechanics and progressively difficult levels make them appealing for short, engaging sessions, perfect for casual gamers on the go.
